About

I'm a final-year Applied Mathematics student at the University of Dhaka, working at the intersection of classical numerical analysis and scientific machine learning. My work starts from a simple obsession: methods should not only reach the right answer, they should let you prove how many digits of that answer you can trust.

I developed two novel derivative-free root-finding formulas — the Perpendicular and Extended Secant methods — now published in a Q1 Elsevier journal with stochastic CESTAC / CADNA validation. Alongside this, I work on physics-informed neural networks and the Homotopy Analysis Method for Volterra and Hammerstein integral equations with discontinuous kernels, as well as mathematical modeling of eco-epidemiological and supply–demand systems.

I currently collaborate as a remote contributor with the Numerical Analysis group at TU Delft and intern with OIST on moment-based population dynamics.
